Loading Data

Using the data included in the india_foot_height.dat file. First column corresponds to Feet height (in cm) and the second to total Height (in cm) of a sample of Indian people.

india_foot_height <- read.table("india_foot_height.dat")
names(india_foot_height)[1:2] <- c("Feet", "Height")

Plotting Data

By creating a scatter plot of dependent variable Height and the indipendent variable Feet we observe a positive relation between these. - The relation does not depend on the units of measure of the variables (changes in the density of axis does not change the relation) - This relation holds even we reverse the plots axis (by making Feet the dependent variable) - If we square or take the log of one of our variables, the relation appears to change.

plot(india_foot_height$Feet, india_foot_height$Height)

Calculating r

The value 0.578 of r for out sample indicate a positive linear relation of our variables. - We perform a t-test with H0: = 0 and H1: != 0. - Based on the resulted p-value and confidence interval we can reject the H0 hypothesis.

cor(india_foot_height$Feet, india_foot_height$Height)
## [1] 0.5779759
cor.test(india_foot_height$Feet, india_foot_height$Height)
